Weekly AI & FinTech Innovation Roundup: January 28-30, 2026

Weekly Roundup Agentic AI Banking Fintech

Highlights this week:

Experimentation to Value Capture — Lloyds came out and said £50 million. Real money. Not a pilot, not a projection — actual value they can point to. They're targeting £100 million next year. That shift from experimentation to accountability? That's the story.

Readiness is the New Competitive Advantage — Meanwhile, Mastercard launched a whole suite to help businesses figure out agentic AI. Their line: "Readiness is the new competitive advantage." I'd push back slightly — readiness without execution is just expensive preparation. But the signal is clear. The big players aren't waiting for perfect anymore.

Lost in Thinking — AI agents can now talk to each other through protocols like A2A. But here's the catch — they still can't think together. They coordinate like strangers at a conference exchanging business cards, not like a team solving a problem. Autonomous execution and collaborative reasoning? Turns out those are completely different challenges.

Mark this Up — One more thing worth watching: Factify just raised $73 million — one of the biggest seed rounds ever — to replace PDFs. Three trillion PDFs floating around, and they're betting the format itself is broken for an AI-first world. Bold. Possibly right
